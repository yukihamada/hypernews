## Hunter Research Checklist

For each candidate, verify:

- [ ] Follower count (500+ minimum)
- [ ] Recent activity (hunted in last 30 days)
- [ ] Niche alignment (AI, dev tools, news, indie)
- [ ] Engagement rate (avg comments/upvotes on hunted products)
- [ ] Accessibility (Twitter, email, or PH DM open)
- [ ] Reputation (no spam, quality products only)

---

## Backup Plan (Self-Hunt)

If no hunter commits:

1. **Self-hunt as maker**: Product Hunt allows this
2. **Amplify with community**: Ask friends/followers to upvote
3. **Focus on comments**: Engage heavily in discussions
4. **Cross-promote**: Share on Twitter, HN, Reddit simultaneously
5. **Quality over hunter clout**: A good product will rise regardless

**Self-Hunt Advantages**:
- Full control over timing and messaging
- More authentic (maker-driven)
- No coordination overhead
- Can still succeed (many top products are self-hunted)

### What Hunters Get

## Red Flags to Avoid

Don't work with hunters who:
- Ask for payment (violates PH rules)
- Have low engagement on previous hunts
- Hunt too frequently (spam behavior)
- Don't align with your product niche
- Are unresponsive or flaky
- Have negative reputation in community

---

## Success Metrics

A good hunter will:
- [ ] Have 500+ followers
- [ ] Have hunted 5+ products in your category
- [ ] Get 100+ upvotes per hunt (average)
- [ ] Engage in comments on their hunted products
- [ ] Be responsive to outreach (reply within 48h)
- [ ] Show genuine interest in your product

---

## Alternative: Community Launch

Instead of a single hunter, mobilize your community:

**Channels**:
1. **Twitter**: Tweet thread + ask for upvotes
2. **Indie Hackers**: Post in "Share Your Project"
3. **Reddit**: r/SideProject, r/InternetIsBeautiful
4. **Hacker News**: "Show HN: news.xyz"
5. **Dev.to**: Write a blog post about the tech
6. **LinkedIn**: Share the story with professional network

**Community Launch Advantages**:
- Broader reach (not dependent on one person)
- More authentic engagement
- Multiple traffic sources
- Builds long-term audience

---

**Remember**: A good hunter amplifies your launch, but a great product succeeds regardless. Focus on quality first, hunter second.
